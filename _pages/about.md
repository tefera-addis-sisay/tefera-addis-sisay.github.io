Tefera Addis Sisay is a Lecturer at [Bahir Dar University](https://www.bdu.edu.et/), Ethiopia. He holds an MEng in Information Systems Technology and Design (Cybersecurity) from the [Singapore University of Technology and Design (SUTD)](https://www.sutd.edu.sg), an MSc in Computer Science (Cybersecurity) from the [University of Trento](https://www.unitn.it/en), and a BSc in Software Engineering from [Bahir Dar University](https://www.bdu.edu.et/), Ethiopia.

His research focuses on secure and intelligent communication systems, with emphasis on AI-driven networking, O-RAN security, anomaly detection, and performance analysis in next-generation mobile networks using Non-RT RIC architectures. He works on intelligent networked systems, cyber-physical infrastructure security, and data-driven network automation.

---

## Key Research Areas

- AI-enabled network optimization, monitoring, and self-management in O-RAN environments  
- Security analysis and threat detection in wireless, edge, and cloud-native network systems  
- Automated anomaly detection and root-cause diagnosis in large-scale distributed systems  
- Trustworthy, explainable, and robust AI for communication and networked infrastructures  
- Cyber-Physical Systems and IoT-enabled intelligent infrastructures  
- Machine learning for networked systems  
- Smartphone security and privacy

## Research

My research focuses on secure, intelligent, and scalable communication systems for next-generation networks. I work on improving the reliability, automation, and resilience of O-RAN and cloud-native network infrastructures through data-driven and AI-enabled approaches.

I leverage machine learning and system intelligence to enhance network observability, enable real-time anomaly detection, and strengthen the security of modern wireless systems.
