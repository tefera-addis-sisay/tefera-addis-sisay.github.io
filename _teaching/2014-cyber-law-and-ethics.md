## Course Description

**Cyber Law and Ethics** explores the legal frameworks, ethical responsibilities, and societal impacts of modern digital technologies. The course introduces students to cybercrime, digital privacy, intellectual property, cybersecurity regulations, online behavior, and ethical decision-making in computing.

Students analyze real-world cyber incidents, legal disputes, and emerging ethical challenges related to artificial intelligence, social media, and digital governance.

---

## Topics Covered

- Introduction to Cyber Law and Digital Governance
- Cybercrime and Computer Misuse
- Privacy, Data Protection, and Digital Rights
- Intellectual Property in Cyberspace
- Ethical Hacking and Responsible Disclosure
- Professional Ethics in Computing
- Social Media Ethics and Online Responsibility
- Artificial Intelligence and Ethical Challenges
- Cybersecurity Policies and Regulations
- Digital Evidence and Cyber Investigation
- International Cyber Law and Global Challenges

---

## Learning Outcomes

By the end of the course, students will be able to:

- Understand the legal foundations of cyberspace and information systems.
- Analyze ethical issues in cybersecurity and computing practices.
- Evaluate real-world cyber incidents using legal and ethical perspectives.
- Apply professional codes of ethics in technology-related decision making.
- Discuss modern challenges related to AI, privacy, surveillance, and digital governance.

---

## Teaching Methodology

The course combines:

- Interactive lectures
- Case-study discussions
- Real-world cyber incident analysis
- Student presentations
- Group debates and discussions
- Research-based assignments

Students are encouraged to critically evaluate contemporary digital issues and develop responsible approaches to technology and cybersecurity.
